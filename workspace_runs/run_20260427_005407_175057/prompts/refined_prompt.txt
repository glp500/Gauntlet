**Task Objective**
Analyze the sales performance across the provided dataset. The goal is to generate a clear, written summary of the key insights and produce one informative figure that visually explains a significant trend or relationship observed in the data (e.g., sales trends over time, or comparison of sales metrics by category).

**Input Dataset Summary**
The single input file is `Warehouse_and_Retail_Sales.csv`. This dataset contains transaction-level records detailing various sales activities, including retail sales, warehouse sales, and retail transfers, linked to specific suppliers and items over time.

**Assumptions From Schema**
1.  **Key Metrics:** The columns `RETAIL SALES`, `RETAIL TRANSFERS`, and `WAREHOUSE SALES` are float-type numerical columns suitable for aggregation (SUM, AVG) and quantitative analysis.
2.  **Grouping Variables:** `YEAR`, `MONTH`, `SUPPLIER`, and `ITEM TYPE` are suitable categorical or temporal dimensions for grouping and segmenting the analysis.
3.  **Temporal Data:** `YEAR` and `MONTH` columns are available for time-series analysis.

**Required Outputs**
1.  **Analysis Summary (Textual):** A comprehensive, well-written summary detailing the overall sales performance, identifying key drivers (e.g., top performing suppliers/item types, seasonal trends, or dominant sales channels).
2.  **Visualization (Figure):** One single, high-quality figure (e.g., a line plot or bar chart) that visually supports the key finding in the summary. Recommended visualization focus: Total sales volume trend over time or total sales comparison across `ITEM TYPE`.

**Code Constraints**
*   Generated code must be contained within the modules: `data_loader.py`, `preprocessing.py`, `analysis.py`, and `figures.py`.
*   Allowed libraries are strictly limited to `pandas` and `matplotlib`.
*   No external network access or shell commands are permitted.
*   The logic implemented must be explicit, step-by-step, and highly readable.
